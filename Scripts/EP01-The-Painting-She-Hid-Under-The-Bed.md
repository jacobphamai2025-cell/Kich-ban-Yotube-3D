# 🎬 SCRIPT — EPISODE 01

## "She Drew 365 Pictures. He Never Saw Any."

---

> **Pillar:** 🔥 Injustice & Redemption
> **Structure:** The Quiet Arc (5 Acts)
> **Duration:** 10 minutes (~1,200 words dialogue + narration)
> **Dialogue/Narrator/Silence:** 65% / 20% / 15%
> **English Level:** A2-B1

---

# CHARACTER SHEET

## Protagonist: LILY (10 tuổi)

| Mục | Chi tiết |
|-----|----------|
| **WANT** | Gặp bố, được bố yêu thương, được bố nhìn thấy tranh |
| **NEED** | Nhận ra mình ĐÃ ĐƯỢC yêu — bởi BÀ — suốt dọc |
| **FLAW** | Nghĩ rằng tình yêu chỉ đến từ người MÌNH chọn yêu (bố), bỏ qua người đang yêu mình (bà) |
| **STRENGTH** | Sáng tạo, kiên nhẫn, không bỏ cuộc dù 365 ngày không hồi âm |
| **HABIT** | Luôn cắn môi khi tập trung vẽ |
| **OBJECT** | Hộp màu cũ (bà mua cho) — gần hết, mòn vẹt |
| **COLOR** | Warm yellow (hope, creativity) |
| **ARCHETYPE** | The Silent Fighter |
| **VOICE** | Soft, young, slightly hesitant, simple sentences |
| **CHANGE** | Từ "vẽ để bố thấy mình" → "vẽ vì mình yêu vẽ, và bà yêu mình" |

## Supporting: GRANDMA ROSE (68 tuổi)

| Mục | Chi tiết |
|-----|----------|
| **Role** | The Anchor + The Invisible Giver |
| **Personality** | Ấm áp, ít nói nhưng mỗi câu đều ý nghĩa, yêu Lily qua HÀNH ĐỘNG không qua lời |
| **Visual** | Tóc bạc búi gọn, apron có vết bột, tay chai sần, nụ cười nhẹ |
| **Voice** | Warm, slow, slightly raspy with age, gentle |
| **Secret** | Giữ tất cả 365 bức tranh — không gửi cho bố vì muốn bảo vệ Lily khỏi bị rejected |

## Absent Character: DAVID (Bố Lily — 35 tuổi)

| Mục | Chi tiết |
|-----|----------|
| **Role** | The Wounded Villain (vắng mặt) |
| **Không xuất hiện trực tiếp** | Chỉ thấy qua: 1 bức ảnh cũ, envelope không mở, giọng narrator mô tả |
| **Backstory** | Bỏ đi khi Lily 2 tuổi. Không phải ghét con — chạy trốn trách nhiệm vì quá trẻ, quá sợ |
| **Voice** | Không có — chỉ xuất hiện qua SỰ VẮNG MẶT |

## Narrator

| Mục | Chi tiết |
|-----|----------|
| **Tone video này** | Tender, slightly sad, warm — like telling a bedtime story about something real |
| **Echo Line** | "It's okay." (xuất hiện 2 lần — khác nghĩa) |

- [x] 💡 ÁNH SÁNG: Ánh sáng vàng chiều = bà luôn ở đó. Tối khi bà mất → sáng trở lại khi mở hộp

|---------|-----------|

# SCRIPT — ENGLISH VERSION

## ACT 1: HOOK & WORLD (0:00 — 1:30)

---

### COLD OPEN (0:00 — 0:10)

**[VISUAL: Close-up of small hands opening an old wooden box. Inside: hundreds of colorful papers. Dust particles float in a beam of golden light.]**

**[SFX: Creak of box opening. Silence.]**

**NARRATOR (V.O.):**
"Some gifts... are only found when the person who gave them... is already gone."

**[BEAT — 2 seconds silence. Cut to black.]**

---

### WORLD SETUP (0:10 — 0:45)

**[VISUAL: Wide establishing shot. A small house at the end of a quiet street. Late afternoon. Golden light. A bicycle leans against the fence. Wind chimes move gently.]**

**[SFX: Distant birds. Wind chimes. Gentle ambient.]**

**NARRATOR (V.O.):**
"This is the story of Lily. She was ten years old. She lived with her grandmother... in a house that always smelled like fresh bread and old paint."

**[VISUAL: Camera slowly pushes toward the house. Through the window, we see a small figure sitting at a table, drawing.]**

---

### CHARACTER INTRO (0:45 — 1:30)

**[VISUAL: Interior. Lily sits by the window. Warm light falls across her face. She's painting carefully. Her box of colors is old, worn down to stubs.]**

**[SFX: Soft pencil/brush strokes on paper.]**

**NARRATOR (V.O.):**
"Every day after school... Lily painted a picture. And every night... she put it in an envelope."

**[VISUAL: Lily finishes a painting — a bright yellow sun with the word "DADDY" written below. She carefully folds it, slides it into an envelope.]**

**[VISUAL: She writes on the envelope: "To David. From Lily." Her handwriting is messy but careful.]**

**NARRATOR (V.O.):**
"She had been doing this... for three hundred and sixty-five days."

**[VISUAL: Lily hands the envelope to Grandma Rose, who is washing dishes. Grandma takes it with a gentle smile.]**

**LILY:**
"Grandma... do you think he'll write back this time?"

**[VISUAL: Grandma Rose pauses. Her smile stays but her eyes shift — just for a moment.]**

**GRANDMA ROSE:**
"We'll see, sweetheart. We'll see."

**[VISUAL: Lily nods and runs upstairs. Grandma watches her go. Then looks down at the envelope. Her smile fades — just slightly.]**

**[SFX: Lily's footsteps fading. Clock ticking.]**

---


## ACT 2: BUILD UP (1:30 — 4:00)

---

### CHARACTER DEPTH (1:30 — 2:30)

**[VISUAL: Morning. Kitchen. Grandma Rose makes breakfast. Lily sits at the table with a half-finished painting. Toast pops up. Grandma places it on Lily's plate — cuts off the crust, the way Lily likes.]**

**LILY:**
"Grandma... what does Daddy look like now?"

**GRANDMA ROSE:**
*(placing plate down, not looking up)*
"Well... he has your eyes. Brown. Like warm chocolate."

**LILY:**
"Does he still have the blue jacket? The one in the photo?"

**GRANDMA ROSE:**
"I don't know, honey. That photo is... very old."

**[VISUAL: Insert shot — an old framed photo on the shelf. A young man (David, mid-20s) holding baby Lily. His face is slightly blurry. Blue jacket.]**

**LILY:**
"I painted him in the blue jacket. In today's picture."

*(She holds up the painting — a man in blue standing next to a small girl in yellow.)*

**LILY:**
"Do you think... he'll like it?"

**GRANDMA ROSE:**
*(long pause. She touches Lily's hair gently.)*
"Anyone would like it, Lily. It's beautiful."

**[VISUAL: Lily smiles — bright, full of hope. Grandma smiles back. But when Lily looks away, Grandma's hand tightens slightly on the kitchen towel.]**

**[SFX: Clock ticking. Toast crunching.]**

---

### TENSION SEEDS (2:30 — 3:00)

**[VISUAL: Afternoon. Lily is at school. Other kids are picked up by parents. Lily watches through the school fence — a father lifts his daughter onto his shoulders. She watches. Doesn't move.]**

**NARRATOR (V.O.):**
"Lily never asked why other kids had fathers who came to pick them up. She never complained. She simply... painted harder."

**[VISUAL: Cut to — Lily's bedroom. Walls covered in paintings. All of them feature the same man in a blue jacket. Some are of them holding hands. Some are of a house with three people. Some just say "I MISS YOU DADDY."]**

**[VISUAL: Under the desk — a pile of returned envelopes. Stamped "RETURN TO SENDER." Lily doesn't see this. The camera lingers on it for 3 seconds.]**

**[SFX: Pencil strokes. Quiet.]**

---

### MINI-CONFLICT — Retention Hook (3:00 — 3:30)

**[VISUAL: Dinner time. Lily and Grandma eat soup. Lily stirs it slowly.]**

**LILY:**
"Grandma... my friend Sophie said... her dad sends her postcards. Every month. From different cities."

*(pause)*

**LILY:**
"Why doesn't Daddy send me anything?"

**[VISUAL: Grandma freezes. Spoon stops mid-air. She puts it down carefully.]**

**GRANDMA ROSE:**
"Lily... some people... they love in ways we can't always see."

**LILY:**
*(voice cracking slightly)*
"But I can't SEE him at all, Grandma. I can't see anything."

**GRANDMA ROSE:**
*(reaching across the table, holding Lily's hand)*
"I know, sweetheart. I know."

*(pause — 2 seconds)*

**GRANDMA ROSE:**
"But you are not invisible. Do you hear me? You are NOT invisible."

**[VISUAL: Lily nods. Looks down. A single tear falls into her soup. She wipes it quickly.]**

**LILY:**
*(whisper)*
"It's okay."

**[NOTE: ★ ECHO LINE — First appearance. Meaning: "I'm pretending it doesn't hurt."]**

---

### STATUS QUO CRACK (3:30 — 4:00)

**[VISUAL: Night. Lily is asleep. Camera pans to Grandma Rose in her bedroom. She sits on her bed holding today's envelope — the one Lily gave her.]**

**[VISUAL: Grandma opens the bedside drawer. Inside — stacks and stacks of envelopes. All sealed. All addressed "To David. From Lily." All UNSENT.]**

**[SFX: Drawer opening. Paper rustling. Grandma's slow breathing.]**

**NARRATOR (V.O.):**
"Three hundred and sixty-five letters. Three hundred and sixty-five paintings. And Grandma Rose... had kept every single one."

**[VISUAL: Grandma places today's envelope on top. Gently. Like placing a flower on a grave. She closes the drawer. Her hand trembles slightly.]**

**[VISUAL: She touches her chest — a subtle gesture of pain. Then stands. Turns off the light.]**

**[SFX: Light switch click. Silence. Only the sound of rain starting outside.]**

**NARRATOR (V.O.):**
"She never sent them. Not one. And Lily... never knew."

**[VISUAL: The window — rain drops begin to fall against the glass. Fade to darker tone.]**

---

## ACT 3: TURN POINT (4:00 — 6:00)

---

### THE INCIDENT (4:00 — 4:45)

**[VISUAL: Time skip indicator — leaves change color outside the window. Autumn. Then winter. Snow on the fence.]**

**NARRATOR (V.O.):**
"Winter came early that year."

**[VISUAL: Interior. Grandma Rose in the kitchen. She coughs — hard. Grabs the counter. Her hand shakes. Lily is at school.]**

**[VISUAL: Cut to — Hospital hallway. Cold blue light. Lily sits on a plastic chair. Her feet don't touch the floor. She holds a small painting — today's painting. For Grandma this time. It shows two people under a yellow sun.]**

**[SFX: Hospital ambient. Beeping machines. Distant footsteps.]**

**[VISUAL: A NURSE kneels in front of Lily.]**

**NURSE:**
"Lily... your grandmother is sleeping now. She's very tired."

**LILY:**
"Can I give her this? I made it for her."

**NURSE:**
*(gently)*
"Of course you can, sweetheart."

**[VISUAL: Lily walks into the room. Grandma lies in bed. Eyes closed. Breathing slow. Machines beep softly.]**

**[VISUAL: Lily places the painting on Grandma's bedside table. Leans in close.]**

**LILY:**
*(whisper)*
"Grandma... please wake up soon. I need you to send my letter tomorrow."

**[VISUAL: She holds Grandma's hand. Grandma's fingers close — barely — around Lily's. Then release.]**

**[SFX: Heart monitor — steady beep... beep... beep...]**

---

### SILENCE BEAT (4:45 — 4:50)

**[VISUAL: Wide shot of the hospital room. Lily small in the chair. Grandma still. Rain on the window.]**

**[AUDIO: COMPLETE SILENCE — 5 seconds. No music. No narration. Only the faint beep.]**

---

### REACTION (4:50 — 5:30)

**[VISUAL: Cut to — The small house. Empty. Dark. No lights on. No smell of bread.]**

**NARRATOR (V.O.):**
*(very soft, very slow)*
"Grandma Rose passed away... on a Tuesday morning. The house was quiet after that. A different kind of quiet."

**[VISUAL: Lily sits alone at the kitchen table. Grandma's chair is empty. The apron still hangs on its hook. A pot sits unwashed on the stove.]**

**[SFX: Clock ticking. Nothing else.]**

**[VISUAL: Lily stares at an envelope in her hands. She's written: "To David. From Lily." But there's no one to give it to now.]**

**LILY:**
*(to herself, barely audible)*
"Who's going to send my letters now?"

**[VISUAL: She puts the envelope down. Pushes it away. Her face — not crying. Just... still. Empty. Bậc 3 — Visible: eyes glistening but not falling.]**

---

### DECISION (5:30 — 6:00)

**[VISUAL: Days pass. Lily doesn't paint. The desk is empty. Colors untouched. Window curtain half-closed — less light in the room now.]**

**NARRATOR (V.O.):**
"For the first time in three hundred and sixty-five days... Lily didn't paint."

**[VISUAL: She's cleaning Grandma's room. Slowly. Mechanically. Folding clothes. Touching things.]**

**[VISUAL: She stops. Looks under Grandma's bed. Something is there. The corner of a wooden box — old, dusty.]**

**[VISUAL: Lily pulls it out. Heavy. She sits on the floor. Looks at it.]**

**[SFX: Box scraping on floor. Dust particles visible in light from window.]**

**NARRATOR (V.O.):**
"And then... she found it."

**[VISUAL: Close-up on Lily's face. Curiosity. Fear. Her fingers on the latch.]**

**[SFX: Click of the latch opening.]**

**[CUT TO BLACK — End of Act 3.]**

---


## ACT 4: CLIMB & CLIMAX (6:00 — 8:30)

---

### ESCALATION (6:00 — 6:45)

**[VISUAL: Lily opens the box. Inside — hundreds of envelopes. HER envelopes. All of them. "To David. From Lily." Stacked neatly. Some yellowed with age.]**

**[SFX: Paper rustling. Lily's breathing quickens.]**

**LILY:**
*(whispering, shaking)*
"These are... these are mine. These are ALL mine."

**[VISUAL: She picks them up — one by one. Faster. Counting. Her hands tremble.]**

**LILY:**
"One... five... twenty... fifty..."

**[VISUAL: She pulls them out — paintings spill across the floor. The yellow suns, the blue jackets, the "I MISS YOU DADDY" — all here. All UNSENT.]**

**LILY:**
*(voice rising — Bậc 4: Dramatic)*
"She never sent them. Grandma... you NEVER sent them?"

**[VISUAL: Lily sits surrounded by 365 paintings on the floor. A sea of color around her. But her face — devastation.]**

**NARRATOR (V.O.):**
"Three hundred and sixty-five days of hope. Three hundred and sixty-five days of waiting. And the letters... never left this house."

---

### DARK MOMENT (6:45 — 7:15)

**[VISUAL: Lily grabs a painting — crumples it. Then another. Her hands shake with anger.]**

**LILY:**
*(Bậc 4 — crying now, angry)*
"Why? WHY did you keep them? He never... he never even KNEW I was painting for him!"

**[VISUAL: She throws a handful of envelopes. They scatter. She pulls her knees to her chest. Buries her face.]**

**LILY:**
*(muffled, sobbing)*
"I waited... every single day. And he didn't even KNOW."

**[VISUAL: The room is dark — curtain still half-closed. Paintings everywhere on the floor like fallen leaves. Lily curled up in the middle. Smallest she's ever looked.]**

**[SFX: Her crying — not loud, just shaking breath and small sobs.]**

**[MUSIC: Piano — single low note. Sustained. Aching.]**

---

### REALIZATION (7:15 — 7:45)

**[VISUAL: Lily, still on the floor. She reaches to push herself up — and her hand lands on something different. A piece of paper that is NOT her painting. Different handwriting. Grandma's handwriting.]**

**[VISUAL: Close-up — a folded letter tucked at the very bottom of the box. Old, careful handwriting. Lily opens it slowly.]**

**[SFX: Paper unfolding. Silence.]**

**LILY:**
*(reading aloud, voice shaking — slow, word by word)*

"My dearest Lily..."

*(pause)*

"I kept your paintings. All of them. I know you will be angry. I know you will not understand. But I could not send them. Because... he returned the first three. Unopened."

*(Lily's breath catches)*

"I could not let you feel that, my love. I could not let you paint your heart... and have it sent back like it was nothing."

**[VISUAL: Lily's face — tears rolling, but she's not angry anymore. She's UNDERSTANDING.]**

**LILY:**
*(continues reading, voice breaking)*

"Your paintings are not nothing. They are EVERYTHING. They are the most beautiful things I have ever seen. So I kept them. Every one. Under my bed. Close to me. Every night... I slept above three hundred and sixty-five pieces of your love."

---

### CLIMAX (7:45 — 8:15)

**[VISUAL: Lily holds the letter to her chest. Eyes closed. Tears streaming. Bậc 5 — EXPLOSIVE but quiet. Not screaming — just FEELING everything at once.]**

**LILY:**
*(reading the last line, barely a whisper)*

"You were never invisible, Lily. I saw you. Every single day. Love, Grandma."

**[VISUAL: ★ EMOTIONAL GAZE — Lily looks up. Directly at camera. Tears on her face. But something in her eyes has shifted. Not broken anymore. SEEN.]**

**[BEAT — 3 seconds. Hold on her face.]**

**[VISUAL: She looks down at the paintings on the floor. Slowly picks one up. Unfolds the one she crumpled. Smooths it gently. Places it back in the box.]**

**LILY:**
*(whisper — to Grandma, to the empty room)*
"It's okay."

**[NOTE: ★ ECHO LINE — Second appearance. Meaning CHANGED: "I understand now. I forgive you. I'm truly okay."]**

---

### FLASH FORWARD PAYOFF (8:15 — 8:30)

**[VISUAL: The same shot from the COLD OPEN — hands opening the box. But NOW we understand. The golden light. The hundreds of papers. It was THIS moment.]**

**[VISUAL: Pull back to reveal — it's Lily. On Grandma's bedroom floor. Surrounded by love she never knew was there.]**

**[SFX: A single wind chime from outside. Like Grandma saying hello.]**

---


## ACT 5: RESOLVE & ECHO (8:30 — 10:00)

---

### NEW NORMAL (8:30 — 9:00)

**[VISUAL: Days later. The house — different now. Curtains open. Light floods in. Lily sits at her desk again. Painting.]**

**[VISUAL: But this time — she's not painting a man in a blue jacket. She's painting a woman with white hair. Smiling. With an apron.]**

**[SFX: Brush strokes. Birds outside. Warmth.]**

**[VISUAL: The wall — some old "Daddy" paintings remain. But new ones are appearing. Grandma making bread. Grandma and Lily at the table. The house in golden light.]**

**NARRATOR (V.O.):**
"Lily still painted every day. But now... the paintings were different."

---

### MOTIF ECHO (9:00 — 9:20)

**[VISUAL: Lily finishes a painting. She holds it up to the light from the window — the SAME window where she always painted. But now the curtain is fully open. Golden light pours in.]**

**[VISUAL: She doesn't put it in an envelope. Instead — she tapes it to the wall. Right next to the window. Where the light hits it every afternoon.]**

**[SFX: Tape pulling. Paper settling on wall. Wind chime outside — gentle.]**

**[VISUAL: Wide shot — the wall of paintings. Old and new. A timeline of a girl who was always loved.]**

---

### NARRATOR REFLECTION (9:20 — 9:40)

**[VISUAL: Golden hour. The house exterior. The bicycle. The wind chimes moving. Everything the same — but lighter somehow.]**

**NARRATOR (V.O.):**
*(warm, slow, gentle)*
"She never did meet her father. Maybe one day she will. Maybe she won't."

*(pause — 2 seconds)*

"But Lily learned something that winter. That love... doesn't always come from where you're looking. Sometimes... it's been under your bed the whole time. Holding you up... while you sleep."

---

### FINAL IMAGE (9:40 — 9:50)

**[VISUAL: Lily's bedroom. Night. She's asleep. Camera slowly pans DOWN — under the bed. The wooden box is there. Closed now. But a warm golden glow seems to come from it — subtle, magical, like the love inside is still alive.]**

**[HOLD — 5 seconds. No sound except soft breathing.]**

---

### CTA (9:50 — 10:00)

**NARRATOR (V.O.):**
*(very soft)*
"If this story stayed with you... you're not alone. Every person has a story worth hearing. See you next time."

**[VISUAL: Fade to black. End screen with channel logo and suggested videos.]**

---

**NARRATOR:**

"Cô bé đã làm điều này... suốt ba trăm sáu mươi lăm ngày."

**LILY:**
"Bà ơi... bà nghĩ lần này bố sẽ viết thư lại cho con không?"

**BÀ ROSE:**
"Rồi sẽ biết thôi, cưng à. Rồi sẽ biết."

---

## HỒI 2: XÂY DỰNG (1:30 — 4:00)

---

### CHIỀU SÂU NHÂN VẬT (1:30 — 2:30)

**LILY:**
"Bà ơi... bây giờ bố trông như thế nào?"

**BÀ ROSE:**
"À... bố có đôi mắt giống con. Nâu. Như sô-cô-la ấm."

**LILY:**
"Bố còn mặc áo khoác xanh không? Cái trong ảnh ấy?"

**BÀ ROSE:**
"Bà không biết, cưng. Bức ảnh đó... cũ lắm rồi."

**LILY:**
"Con vẽ bố mặc áo xanh. Trong bức tranh hôm nay."
*(Giơ bức tranh lên — người đàn ông áo xanh đứng cạnh cô bé áo vàng.)*
"Bà nghĩ... bố sẽ thích không?"

**BÀ ROSE:**
*(im lặng lâu. Tay nhẹ chạm tóc Lily.)*
"Ai cũng sẽ thích, Lily. Nó đẹp lắm."

---

### HẠT GIỐNG CĂNG THẲNG (2:30 — 3:00)

**NARRATOR:**
"Lily không bao giờ hỏi tại sao những đứa trẻ khác có bố đến đón. Cô bé không phàn nàn. Cô chỉ... vẽ nhiều hơn."

**[HÌNH ẢNH: Dưới bàn — chồng phong bì bị trả lại. Đóng dấu "RETURN TO SENDER." Lily không thấy.]**

---

### XUNG ĐỘT NHỎ (3:00 — 3:30)

**LILY:**
